Questions

What does the AI Safety Working Group actually do?

The Working Group is a coordination layer for advocacy organizations. The core activities: joint statements signed by orgs across the political spectrum (breadth of coalition is the whole point), coordinated lobbying so organizations aren't working at cross-purposes, and shared intelligence on which legislative moments matter most. In a past life I worked on political depolarization — the design principle here is that a letter signed by both a progressive org and a conservative one is orders of magnitude more persuasive than fifty letters from orgs that all look the same.

Why does political diversity in the coalition matter?

AI safety is genuinely a cross-partisan issue — but most of the current advocacy infrastructure is concentrated in one part of the political spectrum. A conservative senator dismissing AI risk letters from progressive orgs is entirely predictable. A letter co-signed by orgs their constituents respect is not. Coalition breadth isn't just optics — it's the mechanism by which advocacy actually reaches the people who need to be moved.

What kind of calls to action will individuals receive?

Timely, specific, high-signal actions only. Examples: if a key AI safety bill is coming to a vote and your representative is on the fence, we'll ask you to call their office with a specific script. If there's a candidate in a competitive race who has taken AI risk seriously, we'll flag it before the election. We'll also occasionally share donation asks — curated lists of orgs where your dollars are particularly useful at that moment. We won't spam you with things you can't influence.

When will this actually launch?

We're treating this as a credible-commitment mechanism: we'll only launch the Working Group in earnest once enough organizations have registered interest to make it worth convening. Same for the individual CTA list — we'll wait until we have enough geographic coverage to run targeted campaigns. The goal is to launch with density, not trickle out with noise. That's why sharing this matters.

Who is behind PromptAction?

PromptAction is a project of humansover.ai. The person behind it has worked on political depolarization and is concerned about the lack of coordinated, cross-partisan AI safety advocacy infrastructure in the US and internationally.
